# Legal Issues

## Using a Business's Logo in Demo Videos

**Question:** Is there a legal issue with including a business's logo in a video if we only send it to them?

**Answer:** No legal issue. Reasons:

1. **Nominative fair use** — referring to the business by name/brand to show them a video *about them* is the same as putting their name in an email.

2. **One-to-one demo only** — no public broadcast, no commercial use of their trademark to sell a competing product. Not passing it off as our own work or implying affiliation.

3. **The video benefits them** — it's essentially a free gift. Hard to claim damage from that.

4. **Australian context** — Trade Marks Act 1995 s122 allows use of a mark to indicate the goods/services *are* those of the trademark owner. That's exactly what we're doing.

**Hard limit:** Do not include their logo in videos sent to *other* businesses or publish them publicly without permission. One-to-one demo = fine.
